# Calm Command - Visual Direction

**Project:** Lewis Insurance Agency OS (InsureFlow CRM)
**Source of truth:** AO Renewal Command Center

## Aesthetic

Calm Command. A dark operations console for an insurance agency. One lime accent on a near-black, slightly blue base. Calm, dense, decisive. The opposite of a colorful SaaS dashboard.

## Where this came from

This system is not invented. It is extracted from the one page the owner already trusts: the AO Renewal Command Center. Every token and rule here describes that page so the rest of the app can be brought up to it. The earlier InsureFlow direction (navy #143A5E with orange, the "Tide" split-screen motif) is superseded for the in-app CRM. See `TURNAROUND-HANDOFF.md` for the reconciliation note on the sign-in page and the marketing site.

## Tone

Primary: clinical and calm. Secondary: certain. The interface speaks in plain, temporal labels (what happened last, what happens next) rather than dry field names.

## Density

Comfortably dense. Rich with fact, with real breathing room between groups. Not a trading terminal, not a marketing page.

## Theme

Dark. Near-black base (#0A0D14) with a faint blue cast. A soft blue glow is allowed on the top hero panel only. Surfaces step up in lightness, never down. Borders are low-contrast lines, not fills.

## Color approach

One accent: lime #BEF264, reserved for the single primary action and for the live focus ring. Status and metadata use restrained pills (blue info, sage success, gold warning, controlled red danger). Color never carries meaning on its own.

## Type

Inter for the interface. Geist Mono for figures and identifiers. The record name is a large bold uppercase H1. Section labels are small, uppercase, letter-spaced, in muted gray. Values are heavier and larger than their labels. Numbers are always tabular.

## Consequence profile

- **What is at stake:** client retention and renewals during a book migration off Auto-Owners. A missed follow-up loses a policy.
- **Severity:** 4 of 5. Money and relationships are on the line, though not life safety.
- **Who is under pressure:** producers and CSRs working renewals against a clock, often inside the last five business days before a renewal date.
- **The stressful moment:** late afternoon, a renewal lapses in three days, and the screen has to say in one glance whether the client was contacted and what to do next.

## The signature pattern

Past, present, future across the top of every record. The AO page does this with three hero cards: what happened last (contact log), where it stands (customer snapshot), what happens next (follow-up). Carry that frame to every record surface in the app.
